# Retail-Data-Analytics-Project

Omnichannel Retail Sales and Inventory Analytics Dashboard using SQL, Python, and Power BI

## Overview

This project focuses on cleaning and analyzing retail sales data using SQL Server, followed by building an interactive dashboard in Power BI to derive business insights.

---

## Dataset

* Source: Cleaned Omni-channel Retail Dataset
* Total Orders: ~5000+
* Domain: Retail / Sales

---

## Data Cleaning Steps

* Checked for NULL values
* Verified duplicate records
* Fixed data type issues (converted Sales & Profit from text to FLOAT in SQL)
* Validated data consistency after import

---

## SQL Business Analysis

Performed SQL-based analysis to extract key business insights:

* Total Orders, Revenue, and Profit calculation
* Average Order Value (AOV)
* Category and Sub-Category analysis
* Geographic (Region-wise) performance
* Time-based sales trends
* Discount impact on revenue and profit
* Profit margin analysis
* Channel (Ship Mode) performance analysis

## Business Insights

### 1. Overall Performance

The total revenue is approximately **2.29 million**, with a total profit of around **286K** from **5000+ orders**.
The business is profitable with a healthy profit margin of around **17%**.

---

### 2. Customer Behavior

The average order value is approximately **458**, indicating moderate customer spending per order.
There is potential to increase revenue by improving basket size.

---

### 3. Category & Revenue Distribution

A significant portion of revenue is contributed by a few key categories, showing strong performance in specific segments.

---

### 4. Sub-Category Insights

Some sub-categories generate high revenue and profit, while others contribute less or may reduce profitability.
This highlights opportunities for optimization.

---

### 5. Geographic Performance

Certain regions contribute more to overall sales, indicating strong market presence, while others show potential for growth.

---

### 6. Discount Impact

Higher discounts can negatively impact profitability, indicating the need for a balanced pricing strategy.

---

### 7. Profitability Analysis

The overall profit margin (~17%) indicates efficient business operations, but there is scope to improve margins further.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Focus on high-performing categories and sub-categories
* Optimize discount strategies to protect profit margins
* Improve performance in low-performing regions
* Increase average order value through bundling or upselling
